Gemfibrozil Compounded Oral Liquid is a medication to help treat hyperlipidemia in your pet. Hyperlipidemia are high levels of cholesterol or triglycerides in the blood and are common in dogs and cats. This condition can be brought on by obesity, diabetes, medication-induced, or genetically hereditary. To manage this condition and lower fats in the blood, your veterinarian may introduce a low fat, high-fiber diet along with lipid (fat) lowering medication such as gemfibrozil.
Gemfibrozil is a prescription drug that belongs to a group of medications known as fibrates which work by decreasing the amount of fat that the liver produces.
Generally, there are no obvious signs of hyperlipidemia, and your vet will often detect this condition with routine blood work. If clinical signs do appear they are often gastrointestinal related such as diarrhea, vomiting, pancreatitis, or even skin itching or hair loss. Treating high triglycerides is important to avoid worsting conditions such as seizures, pancreatitis, liver disease, or behavioral changes. Depending on the cause of this condition in your pet will determine how your veterinarian chooses to treat it.

Uses

Gemfibrozil Compounded Oral Liquid is given by mouth in liquid form twice a day, every 12 hours, 30 minutes before a meal on an empty stomach. Gemfibrozil will be prescribed by your veterinarian and added as part of the overall treatment when diet modifications alone have not been successful.
This medication should be given just as your veterinarian prescribes. It must be measured carefully, and it's best to use the syringe provided to ensure you are able to give the proper dosage required. After dosing your pet, rinse and dry the syringe, and screw the cap tightly onto the bottle.
If you have difficulty giving it to your pet, contact your veterinarian for some helpful tips. We recommend washing your hands with soap and water after handling any medication.

Possible Side Effects

Gemfibrozil Compounded Oral Liquid is generally safe. The most common gemfibrozil side effects include nausea, vomiting, or diarrhea.
More severe adverse effects may be low energy levels, abdominal pain, loss of appetite, yellowing of the eyes, skin, or gums.Contact your veterinarian immediately if these side effects continue or become more severe.

Drug & Food Interactions

Drug Interactions: If your pet is on any of the following medications you should use gemfibrozil with caution:

  • Colchicine - When combined may increase the risk of myopathy.
  • Statins - (e.g., atorvastatin, simvastatin): When combined may increase the risk of myopathy.
  • Thiazide diuretics, beta-blockers, and estrogens - When combined may increase triglyceride concentrations.
  • Ursodiol - When combined may reduce the effectiveness of gemfibrozil.
  • Warfarin - When combined gemfibrozil may potentiate anticoagulant effects.

Precautions

Gemfibrozil Compounded Oral Liquid should not be given to pets that are allergic to it or have severe liver or kidney problems or dysfunction.

Monitoring

Your veterinarian will likely monitor your pets plasma triglycerides, adverse effects, liver enzymes, and liver function tests will be conducted.
Gemfibrozil withdrawal symptoms can include an increase in blood cholesterol levels. Your veterinarian may want your pet to follow a special diet to prevent this from happening.

Instructions

Instructions

Gemfibrozil Compounded Oral Liquid is usually given to your pet by mouth two times per day, 30 minutes prior to eating breakfast or an evening meal. Gemfibrozil is absorbed better on an empty stomach. Exact instructions will be prescribed by your veterinarian.

Storage Instructions

Gemfibrozil Compounded Oral Liquid should be stored at room temperatures from 68° - 77°F with the cap tightly closed, and away from light, moisture, heat, or humidity.

Doses

Gemfibrozil Compounded Oral Liquid should be given by mouth two times per day 30 minutes before a meal. The maximum daily dose of gemfibrozil in dogs is 150-300 mg per dog every 12 hours. The maximum daily dose of gemfibrozil for cats is 7.5 – 10 mg every 12 hours. The exact dosage will be prescribed by your veterinarian.

What if I miss giving my pet a dose? If you miss a dose, give the next dose as soon as possible. If it is almost time for the following dose, then continue with the next scheduled one and skip the missed dose. Do not give a double dose to catch up or make up for a missed dose.

FAQ

What is gemfibrozil?

Gemfibrozil is the generic medication for the brand name Lopid. It is a medication used together with a healthy low fat diet to help reduce fats, triglycerides in the blood.

What is gemfibrozil used for in dogs?

Gemfibrozil is a medication used to help treat high cholesterol and hyperlipidemia in the blood. It lowers cholesterol and lipid levels which can reduce the risk of heart disease and other conditions.

Can gemfibrozil cause weight loss?

Gemfibrozil is usually prescribed with a low-fat diet to treat high cholesterol and fat levels in the blood. Some pets might experience weight loss, however, it would be from a combination of a healthy diet, lifestyle, and the medication. Treating this can help prevent pancreatitis and other heart problems.

What is gemfibrozil 600 mg?

Gemfibrozil comes in tablets of 600 mg, 300 mg or compounded liquid form.

What are the side effects of gemfibrozil?

Some side effects are an upset stomach, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and constipation.

Is gemfibrozil a statin?

Gemfibrozil is the generic medication for Lopid. It is a fibrate that helps treat high triglycerides and cholesterol problems. They work differently than statins.

Is gemfibrozil a fibrate?

Gemfibrozil is in a group of medications called fibrates which works to reduce the level of triglycerides in the liver.

Gemfibrozil and statin interaction?

It is not recommended to use simvastatin and gemfibrozil together. When combined it can cause liver damage.

What is the gemfibrozil mechanism of action?

Gemfibrozil is a fibrate that lowers serum triglycerides and low density lipoprotein cholesterol. It benefits by increasing high density lipoproteins cholesterol. It binds to PPAR-alpha which increases fatty acid oxidation and reduces triglycerides levels.

What if my pet overdoses?

If you know or suspect that your pet experienced an overdose, call veterinary services immediately or a 24-hour poison consultation center should be contacted. Signs of an overdose can include panting, vomiting, tremors, vocalization, and increased heart rate.
